Space Missions

Space missions encompass robotic and crewed expeditions across the solar system and beyond, managed by agencies like NASA, ESA, JAXA, Roscosmos, and private partners. These include Earth-observers, planetary explorers, telescopes, and human spaceflight—with missions in development, in-progress, and groundbreaking successes spanning decades. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Legacy & Achievements

Mission Categories

NASA’s mission programs include Earth science (Aqua, Aura, NISAR), heliophysics (PUNCH), planetary science (Lucy, Psyche, DAVINCI), astrobiology, commercial lunar (CLPS), human spaceflight (Artemis, Commercial Crew), and space telescopes (Hubble, JWST). :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2} These programs support exploration, climate research, deep-space astrophysics, and preparation for crewed missions to the Moon and Mars.



Highlighted Missions

• **Artemis II**: First crewed mission orbiting the Moon, testing spacecraft systems for future lunar landings. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
• **PUNCH**: A quartet of microsatellites to image the solar corona and how it connects to the heliosphere, launched March 12, 2025. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
• **Lucy**: Continuing its exploration of Trojan asteroids in Jupiter’s orbit through 2033, following its April 20, 2025 flyby. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
• **Psyche**: A Discovery mission to a metallic asteroid, launching 2023 and arriving 2029. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}



Upcoming Launches (2025–2027)

CLPS lunar landers like Blue Ghost 2, IM‑2 Athena, and Griffin lift off in 2025, along with Mars missions like EscaPADE. Key future missions include Europa Clipper (2030), Artemis III lunar landing (~2027), and Venus probes DAVINCI, VERITAS, ENVISION by 2031–2032. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}



Scientific Goals

Missions aim to understand Earth’s climate and environment, probe planetary atmospheres and geology, hunt for life and water, perform deep-space astrophysics, and establish human presence on the Moon and Mars. Instruments like JWST, Hubble, PUNCH, and NISAR deliver unprecedented data. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}



International & Commercial Partnerships

Collaboration spans agencies across nations and private entities—from ESA and JAXA to SpaceX, Blue Origin, Sierra Space, and Intuitive Machines. Commercial Lunar Payload Services (CLPS) and commercial crew programs like Starliner and Dream Chaser exemplify public–private partnership. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}



Future Outlook

The 2020s will usher in the next wave of exploration: crewed Moon landings (Artemis III), commercial orbital habitats, robotic Mars missions, Venus and asteroid probes, and astronomical observatories. Missions to Triton, Trojan asteroids, and beyond promise to deepen our understanding of the solar system and prepare for human missions to Mars. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}
